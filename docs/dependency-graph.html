How to read the graph

  • Hubs. Task adoption (where the technology block converges and the firm block fans out) and job searchers (where every displacement channel pools before the matching bottleneck) are the model's load-bearing joints.
  • Feedback loops. Four cycles make outcomes emergent: imitation (adoption → peer imitation → adoption), demand rebound (adoption → price → demand factor → headcount targets), displacement–congestion (layoffs → searchers → reservation wage → wages → adoption ROI), and the small discouragement cycle (searchers ↔ discouragement).
  • Policy periphery. Each policy lever touches a single edge of the densely cyclic core — the graph-structural reason the paper finds policy effects seed- and specification-sensitive.
  • Missing loop. Outcomes have no outgoing edges; in particular nothing feeds back from unemployment into demand — the absent aggregate-demand channel listed in the paper's limitations.

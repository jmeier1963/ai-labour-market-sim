Model Logic

Capability path. AI performance grows exponentially at the selected annual speed, then maps into usable capability with a saturating curve.

Task substitution. Each sector has exposed tasks, a maximum substitution share, complementarity, and a threshold that delays adoption until capability and ROI are high enough.

Non-linearity. Adoption is an S-curve with spillovers. Once adoption raises ROI in adjacent sectors, exposed task automation can jump quickly.

Labour market tipping. The instability threshold is crossed when displacement pressure plus transition unemployment exceeds the economy's reallocation capacity.

Starting points: Anton Korinek, Economic Policy Challenges for the Age of AI; Korinek and Suh, Scenarios for the Transition to AGI; Hampole, Papanikolaou, Schmidt and Seegmiller, Artificial Intelligence and the Labor Market.
